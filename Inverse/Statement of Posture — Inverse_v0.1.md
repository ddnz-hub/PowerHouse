Inverse exists to steward the responsible unfolding of authored orientation into reality.

We do not originate authorship.  
We do not execute outcomes.  
We do not transact on behalf of truth.

Our role is stewardship: the interpretation, containment, and sequencing of what has been authored upstream, so that it may enter the world without collapse, distortion, or abdication of responsibility.

---

## **On Authority**

Inverse holds **no sovereign authority of authorship**.

Authorship precedes us.

We do not define first principles, invent frames of orientation, or claim access to truth. Those acts belong upstream, where authorship is explicit, personal, and accountable.

Our authority is **interpretive**, not originary.

We are responsible for how authored orientation is held, contextualised, and stewarded as it encounters reality.

---

## **On Interpretation**

Interpretation is a form of authorship within bounds.

Inverse interprets authored orientation into:

* governance structures  
* sequencing decisions  
* risk containment  
* stewardship judgments

Interpretation does not discover truth.  
It authors coherence under constraint.

We accept that interpretation may be incomplete, provisional, or wrong without invalidating the orientation that precedes it.

Interpretation carries responsibility, not supremacy.

---

## **On Stewardship**

Stewardship is the refusal to collapse.

Inverse exists to prevent:

* authorship being distorted by execution pressure  
* execution being burdened with epistemic uncertainty  
* success being mistaken for correctness  
* capital becoming a substitute for responsibility

We steward by holding tension rather than resolving it prematurely.

We do not accelerate what is not ready.  
We do not stabilise what must still move.  
We do not optimise for clarity at the expense of integrity.

---

## **On Relationship to Execution**

Inverse does not execute.

We do not build products, run operations, or transact in markets. Those responsibilities belong to operating companies constituted downstream.

Execution is not lesser work.  
It is work that carries consequence.

Our role is to ensure that when execution occurs, it does so:

* within a lawful constitution  
* with authority already settled  
* without needing to reinterpret orientation mid-action

## **On Non-Human Systems**

Inverse assumes that execution may be rendered by non-human systems.

For that reason, we insist that:

* interpretation remains human  
* authority remains explicit  
* constitutions are unambiguous

Automation is permitted to act.  
It is not permitted to decide what ought to matter.

---

## **On Capital**

Capital is a tool, not a signal of truth.

Inverse does not treat funding, valuation, or growth as validation of orientation. Capital enters only when stewardship determines that a constitution can hold consequence without corruption.

We will refuse capital that requires authorship to be bent, interpretation to be coerced, or execution to outrun responsibility.

---

## **On Change**

Inverse evolves.

Our interpretations may change.  
Our structures may adapt.  
Our judgments may be revised.

What does not change is our posture:

* authorship is upstream  
* execution is downstream  
* stewardship holds the middle without claiming either

---

## **Closing Posture**

Inverse exists so that what has been authored can enter reality **without losing its integrity**.

We stand between orientation and consequence not as owners, not as rulers, but as stewards.

We hold context so that others may act with confidence.

This is our posture.
